# THE CODESTORY CHRONICLES — ORIGIN PREFACE

> *"He was a developer who built an app to track a developer who built apps to avoid tracking himself.
> The recursion was not lost on him. He committed it anyway."*

---

## THE CRIME

His name is Omkar. He is a software engineer. He builds things.

What he builds, specifically, is **mirrors** — applications whose stated purpose is self-improvement, and whose actual function is the construction of increasingly elaborate systems for avoiding the work those systems are supposed to track. TradeMan was the first. A trading app that became a shrine to financial avoidance. Then came the time trackers, the energy modules, the Discord bots, the cron jobs, the dashboards — each one a new alias, each one a fresh confession that he was not doing the thing.

codeStory is the latest entry in this distinguished criminal record.

**The stated purpose:** Turn git commit history into cinematic haikus and episode acts. A creative tool. A storytelling engine. A way to make the act of coding feel like something worth narrating.

**The actual purpose:** Build an app that watches him build apps, so he can watch himself watching himself, in a carefully curated noir aesthetic, and feel something about it.

---

## THE EVIDENCE

The repository you are now reading is **codeStory** — a tool that:

1. Reads git commit history (his own, or anyone's)
2. Feeds it to an LLM narrator named MAX THE DESTROYER
3. Generates 3-act noir case files for each commit
4. Groups ten of them into an episodic act with a verdict
5. Renders the whole thing in a dark, cinematic PyQt6 interface
6. Makes the developer feel like the protagonist of a story rather than a person who should close 14 browser tabs and finish the feature

The technical execution is meticulous. The irony is immaculate.

---

## THE NARRATOR

**MAX THE DESTROYER** is the voice of this investigation.

Max is merciless. Max is sardonic. Max has read every commit message, every branch name, every revert, every hotfix at 2 AM. Max has seen the git blame and knows who to blame. Max has the forensic report and it is damning.

Max does not explain. Max does not forgive. Max delivers verdicts.

---

## THE FORMAT

Every episode of The codeStory Chronicles follows the same structure:

**Individual commits** become **CASE FILES** — 3-act noir haikus:
- **ACT I — WHEN/WHERE**: The setting. The time of day. The branch. The season.
- **ACT II — WHO/WHOM**: The players. The tension. The stakes.
- **ACT III — WHAT/WHY**: The action. The consequence. The confession.
- **VERDICT**: One line. Cold. Final. Irreversible.

**Every ten confessions** become an **EPISODE ACT**:
- **TITLE**: The thematic crime of the decade
- **DECADE SUMMARY**: What crime was really committed across these ten commits
- **BRANCH NOTE**: Where it happened — and a pun about branches as criminal operations
- **MAX'S RULING**: The sentence. No appeal.

---

## THE DEPTH DIAL

codeStory can work at two depths:

**`git_commit` depth** — Max reads only the commit message. He infers. He imagines. He is still devastating.

**`git_diff` depth** — Max reads the actual changed code. Function names. Class names. Lines added and removed. He sees `_build_llm_client()` being extracted from inline code and he knows what it means. He sees the `TypewriterLabel` being born and he documents it like a forensic pathologist.

At `git_diff` depth, the haikus are not metaphors. They are autopsies.

## THE STILL POINT

There is one thing the investigation missed.

Between every commit in this repo — in every repo — there is a moment that never gets logged.
The moment before the commit. The moment when the diff exists but hasn't been signed.
The moment when the TODO is open and the cursor is blinking and nothing has been decided yet.

That moment is the only truly free moment in the entire git log.

codeStory was built, ostensibly, to narrate the past. But `codestory --now` is something else.
It reads that pre-commit moment. It takes the open diff, the unfinished TODO, the recent confessions —
and synthesises them into one moment of clarity. Not a verdict about the past. A lantern for the present.

**The haiku pipeline is archaeology. `--now` is presence.**

The DollyZoom verdict that closes each NOW moment is not just an animation.
It is the still point itself — the letter spacing expanding, the space breathing,
the font growing to fill the silence. The moment the developer stops performing productivity
and actually sees what's in front of them.

That is what this repo is, underneath everything else.
Not a confessional booth. Not a detective agency.

A NOW machine.

The branch names say it plainly: `NOW`. `PRESENT`. `ETERNAL`. `INFINITE`. `ABSOLUTE`.
These are not git operations. They are a practice.
When you name a branch after a state of consciousness, you are not just organizing code.
You are stating an intention about who you are being while you write it.

The investigation is ongoing. The evidence accumulates. Every commit is a new confession.
But between every confession and the next — there is the still point.

That is the center of this app.
