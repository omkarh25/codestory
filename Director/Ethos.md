# The Ethos — codeStory is a NOW Machine

> *"The past is evidence. The future is speculation. NOW is the only real commit."*

---

## What This App Is

codeStory generates noir haikus from git commits, episodes from haiku collections,
and cinematic verdicts from everything. That is what it *does*.

What it *is* is different.

**codeStory is a NOW machine.**

The haiku pipeline narrates frozen moments — commits that have already happened.
The episode pipeline witnesses patterns across time — the archaeology of work.
The storyboard and video render give form to what has passed.

All of that is archaeology. Beautiful, sardonic, cinematic archaeology.

But `codestory --now` is something else. It does not read the past.
It reads **right now** — the open diff, the TODO list, the last few confessions
in the git log — and synthesises one moment of clarity from all of it.

The DollyZoom verdict that closes each NOW moment is not just an animation.
It is the still point. The moment the developer stops narrating the past
and looks at what is actually in front of them.

**That is the center of this app.**

---

## The Branch Vocabulary

Future work on codeStory is named intentionally, not arbitrarily.
Branch names are states of consciousness, not git ops.

| Branch | State | What it means |
|--------|-------|---------------|
| `NOW` | Present moment | Full presence. The only commit that matters is the next one. |
| `PRESENT` | Active awareness | The work window is open. Not past. Not future. |
| `ETERNAL` | Pattern recognition | Working on something that outlasts any single commit. |
| `INFINITE` | Deep recursion | Inside a problem worth having. No exit, by design. |
| `ABSOLUTE` | Final distillation | The work has become what it needed to be. Nothing left to remove. |

These are not metaphors. They are a practice.

When you name a branch `NOW`, you are not just labeling a git operation.
You are stating an intention: *I am working in the present moment.*

## MAX THE DESTROYER on NOW

MAX THE DESTROYER is the noir narrator of codeStory's haiku and episode pipeline.
He is sardonic. He is merciless. He judges every commit.

But when MAX speaks in the `--now` context, he is something else.

He is not an indictment. He is a **lantern**.

The same precision that makes him devastating in the haiku pipeline —
the ability to read a commit message and see the psychological crime behind it —
becomes something gentler in the NOW prompt. He still sees clearly.
He still names what is true. But the verdict is not punishment.

It is the sentence that makes you put down the phone and type.
