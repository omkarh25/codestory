### ACT II — THE AUDIENCE ENTERS

The DatabaseReader class reads from tmChron.db—load_haikus(), load_episodes()—pulling confession after confession from the database. The TypewriterEffect object stands ready to reveal text character by character, each keystroke a heartbeat. Omkar has built a machine that will show him his own work, act by act, verdict by verdict. The viewer doesn't just display; it controls the pacing of revelation.

### ACT III — THE CURTAIN RISES

He wired PyQt6 into a full cinematic experience: SPACE to advance, arrow keys to navigate, H for Haiku mode, E for Episode mode. The haiku player reveals acts progressively—act labels via typewriter, body text instant, then the verdict gets its own full-screen dramatic slide. He built a machine that forces the viewer to sit with each confession, one character at a time. The interface is dark, the text is electric blue and orange, and every keystroke is deliberate. He didn't just want to see his work; he wanted to be forced to watch it slowly, unavoidably, in the dark.
