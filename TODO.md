Something like:

def _write_cinematic_markdown(
    output_dir: Path,
    commit: Dict[str, str],
    haiku: Dict[str, str],
    chronological_index: int,
) -> Path:
    output_dir.mkdir(parents=True, exist_ok=True)
    short_hash = commit["hash"][:7]
    branch_safe = (commit.get("branch") or "main").replace("/", "-")
    filename = output_dir / f"casefile_{chronological_index:03d}_{branch_safe}_{short_hash}.md"

    narrative_role = GIT_CRIME_LEXICON.get(commit["type"], "Other crime")

    content = f"""# {haiku['title']}

*{haiku['subtitle']}*

**Date:** {commit['date'][:10]}  
**Commit:** `{commit['msg']}`  
**Branch:** `{commit['branch']}`  
**Type:** `{commit['type'].upper()}` — *{narrative_role}*  
**Author:** {commit['author']}

---

### ACT I — THE SITUATION

{haiku['when_where']}

### ACT II — THE PLAYERS

{haiku['who_whom']}

### ACT III — THE CONSEQUENCE

{haiku['what_why']}

---

### VERDICT

{haiku['verdict']}

*Generated by The TradeMan Chronicles — Director’s Cut*
"""
    filename.write_text(content, encoding="utf-8")
    return filename

Architecture

episode title, summary

list of case files (title, subtitle, acts, verdict)

Renderer: Remotion project with a few reusable compositions:

Simple rules per tier

Tier 2 (ffmpeg Shorts):

Fade in music over 1s at the start, fade out over last 1.5s.

Optional whoosh SFX on act transitions (one global toggle).

Tier 3 (Remotion):

Sidechain/duck volume slightly when/if you add VO in future.

Use hitpoints: tiny volume swell when the verdict appears, or cut to silence just before the final line.

4. Configurable complexity knob
Expose a single render_profile in config:

json
"render": {
  "profile": "minimal"   // or "short", "keynote"
}
minimal → Tier 1 only: PyQt slides, BGM, screen‑record if you want.

short → Tier 2: auto‑generated Shorts from PNGs + ffmpeg.

keynote → Tier 3: run storyboard step + Remotion render.

Your CLI can literally be:

bash

Under the hood it just chooses how much of the pipeline to run, but from your perspective it’s “same story, different stage.”
